  • Freezer tips

    Good stuff from @charlotteshucks about using the freezer well.

  • CORM!

    Rob Corr discovers the recipe for Mamasita’s CORM! – the Food of 2010 – in the Fin Review.

  • Win at Thanksgiving

    The late Leslie Harpold's foolproof? Thanksgiving instruction manual. Interesting in an anthropological sense, and also Christmas is a BIT like Thanksgiving I guess.

  • How to Shuck an Oyster

    How to Shuck an Oyster is writer Charlotte Wood's blog about food. I made the labneh recipe not so long ago and it's terrific. I'm going to make her spring cous-cous recipe this weekend.

  • Build you own pizza oven

    These good folks in Brooklyn built their own pizza oven. I want one.

  • Chicken with pears

    As cooked by Christopher Walken, of course. Keep watching to see the nice kitty at the end.

  • The New Vegetarian

    Some lovely vegetarian recipes in here, by Yotam Ottolenghi. I'm drooling right now over this one.

  • Reap the yog(h)urt

    Cultivating a long-term relationship with heirloom yoghurt bacteria.

  • Goat Meat

    Great article in the Times about the growing trend towards eating goat.

  • Official Nomify video

    Nom nom nom nom nom nom nom (thanks Max).

  • Lagavulin Single Malt

    I was lucky enough to get to try a few different single-malt whiskies the other night, one of which was this Lagavulin; another was Aberlour, which was sweeter and stickier (and probably more of a 'junior' whiskey, particularly if you have a sweet tooth).

  • Make your own vanilla extract

    Straightforward recipe, from the always excellent Chocolate and Zucchini.

  • Beginning Cheesemaking

    How to make cheese! Bookmarked for the apocalypse.

  • Viet World Kitchen

    The basics and complexities of Vietnamese cooking explained. This provides excellent information about distinctive Vietnamese ingredients - how they're used and how to make reasonable substitutions.

  • Manjula's kitchen

    YouTube channel of an Indian woman demonstrating the preparation of vegetarian Indian food. Bloody fantastic.

  • Nigella Lawson

    The Domestic Goddess's website isn't as glamourous as her books, but it does have quite a nice way of her fans sharing recipes.

  • Jamie Oliver

    He blogs! He podcasts! Everybody loves Jamie.

  • BBC Food
  • Tasting Menu

    Foodie blog with recipes and general industry news.

  • Delia Online

    Website of the world-famous Delia Smith. Lots of free recipes, plus premium content if you want to fork out.
